How the Allocation Process Works

{{ endblock }} {{ block styles}} {{ endblock }} {{ block content }}

You will now learn the full technical details of the allocation process.

The allocation process begins with a multi-step process which does not involve your own submitted ranking, as follows:

In the first step, each prize is paired to its highest-priority participant, among all participants except for you.

To solve the conflict, only the prize with the highest rank at that participant’s ranking remains paired to that participant. The others get unpaired.

In the next step, possible conflicts are detected and solved.

If two (or more) prizes are paired to the same participant, this is a conflict.

Each conflict is solved in two steps:

  • Unpair: only the prize with the highest rank at that participant’s ranking remains paired to that participant. The others get unpaired.
  • Re-pair: all unpaired prizes can only get re-paired to participants that they were not paired with before. Each unpaired prize is re-paired to its highest-priority participant, among the participants they were not yet paired with and except for you.

Later steps continue in the same way, by detecting and solving new conflicts.

Like before, if two (or more) prizes are paired to the same participant, this is a conflict.

    Like before, each conflict is solved in two steps:
  • Unpair: only the prize with the highest rank at that participant’s ranking remains paired to that participant. The others get unpaired, even if they successfully got paired to that participant in the first step.
  • Re-pair: Every unpaired prize get re-paired to its highest-priority participant, among the participants they were not previously paired with and except for you.

During the process, one prize will encounter a conflict with each participant, except for you, and will get unpaired from all of them. That prize will remain unpaired at the end of the process.

When there are no more conflicts and when one prize was unpaired from all participants, the process is over. The result is each prize, except for the unpaired one, being paired to a different participant, except for you.

Each prize except for the unpaired one is then temporarily allocated to the participant it is paired to.

In this temporary allocation, no prize was allocated to you. To determine which prize is allocated to you, the computer first determines which prizes you can obtain in principle. These are the Obtainable Prizes.

To determine which prize is allocated to you, the computer first determines which prizes you can obtain in principle. These are the Obtainable Prizes.

You can obtain two kinds of prizes:
1. Any prize at which your priority is higher than the participant it is temporarily allocated to.
2. The prize that was left unpaired in the temporary allocation.
You cannot obtain any other prizes.

At the end, among the Obtainable Prizes, you get the one that you ranked the highest.
