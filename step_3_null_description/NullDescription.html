We will now remind you of the general details of the game and allocation process.
This page includes the exact same text you read at the beginning of the study. Please read it again to make sure you understand.

In this study, you and three computerized participants, Ruth, Shirley, and Theresa, are going to play a game for four prizes. Each prize is worth money, but is worth a different amount of money for each participant.

You and the computerized participants will each rank the four prizes from most preferred to least preferred.
Then, an allocation process will use these rankings to allocate the prizes — one prize for each participant.

The allocation process tries to give each participant a prize that they ranked higher rather than a prize that they ranked lower, while taking into account the rankings of all participants.

You will learn about the game and allocation process while playing a round of the game. The text in blue provides explanations about the game and allocation process. Make sure you read it carefully.

A round of the game has three steps.

Step 1: Round Information

In Step 1, you first see the prizes you can get in this round. Each prize is worth money, but might be worth a different amount of money for each participant.

In the table above, under each prize A, B, C or D, you can see how much money it would add to your earnings.

The money amounts shown in your table can be different in different rounds of the game, and they were determined beforehand.
You and the other participants cannot affect the money amounts shown in your table.

In addition, the computerized participants do not know the money amounts shown in your table, and the allocation process is not affected by these money amounts.

Next, you see what we call the prize priorities.

All four participants have some priority at each of the four prizes.

These priorities can affect the allocation of prizes.
The higher your priority is at some prize, the more likely you are to get that prize at the end of the process.

In this round, the prize priorities for you and for the other participants are:

Each column shows the priorities of all participants at some prize, written from highest to lowest.
For example, the column under Prize A shows its priorities. Prize A has Ruth in the 1st priority (the highest), Shirley in the 2nd priority, You in the 3rd priority and Theresa in the 4th priority (the lowest). So, for example, it is easier for Ruth to get Prize A this round, and harder for Theresa to get Prize A this round.

The prize priorities can be different in different rounds of the game, and they were determined beforehand.
You and the other participants cannot affect the prize priorities.

Step 2: Submit Your Ranking

In Step 2, you are asked to rank the four prizes, from most preferred to least preferred.

The computerized participants simultaneously submit their own rankings.

Their rankings are chosen completely at random, with each possible ranking being equally likely. Your own ranking cannot affect the computerized participants’ rankings.

Step 3: Allocation Process

In Step 3, the allocation process allocates the prizes to participants.
