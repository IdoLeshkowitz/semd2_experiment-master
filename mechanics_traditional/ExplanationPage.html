You will now learn the full technical details of the allocation process.

The allocation process is a multi-step process, as follows:

In the first step, each participant is paired to thier highest-rank prize.

If two (or more) participants are paired to the same prize, there is a conflict.

To solve the conflict, only the participant with the highest priority at that prize remains paired to that prize. The others get unpaired.

In the second step, all unpaired participants can only get paired to prizes that they were not paired with before.

Each unpaired participant is paired to thier highest-rank prize, among the prizes they were not yet paired with.

Like before, if two (or more) participants are paired to the same prize as a result, there is a conflict.

To solve the conflict, only the participant with the highest priority at that prize remains paired to that prize. The others get unpaired, even if they successfully got paired to that prize in the first step.

Later steps continue in the same way. Each step, every unpaired participant is paired to thier highest-rank prize, among the prizes they were not previously paired with.

If there are conflicts, only the participant with the highest priority at a prize remains paired to that prize and the others get unpaired, including paired participants from previous steps.

When there are no more conflicts, the process is over. The result is each participant, being paired to a different prize.

Each prize is then allocated to the participant paired to it.
